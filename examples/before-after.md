# Example: The Before/After

## Input

```
/linkedin-post Six months ago I was on every sales call. Now I'm in fewer than 20%. Close rate went up. What changed: I wrote down what I said on every call for a month, turned it into a script, handed it over.
format: before-after
```

## Output

Six months ago I was on every single sales call. Every one. I told myself it was because the product was complex and customers needed a founder on the line.

It wasn't that. I just hadn't written down what I said.

I spent a month documenting every call. Not transcribing — documenting. What I opened with, where I asked questions, what objections came up and how I handled them. Then I turned it into a script and handed it to two people.

Now I'm on fewer than 20% of calls. Our close rate went up.

Turned out it wasn't me closing deals. It was the script. I just hadn't separated the two things long enough to see it.

If you're still on every call, the question isn't whether you're good at sales. It's whether you've written down what good looks like.
