# Example: The Polarising Statement

## Input

```
/linkedin-post Founder-led sales is overrated. The founders who scale fastest are the ones who handed over earliest. The skill isn't selling — it's making selling transferable.
format: polarising
```

## Output

Founder-led sales is overrated.

The founders who scale fastest aren't the ones who stayed in every deal longest. They're the ones who wrote down what they said on every call, turned it into a script, and handed it over at month six. Not month eighteen.

The skill isn't selling. The skill is making selling transferable.

The version of this that doesn't scale: the founder who's still on 80% of demos at Series A because "nobody closes it like I do." They're right. They've also accidentally made themselves the bottleneck in their own go-to-market.

Document the conversation. Hand it over. See what breaks. Fix what breaks. Repeat.

#foundersales #b2bsales #saas
---

**Format used:** The Polarising Statement — a counterintuitive claim followed by evidence and the strongest counterargument, which keeps it from reading as a hot take.
